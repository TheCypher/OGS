Frequently Asked Questions

  • Provided below are brief answers to the most common questions that we would like to answer for you that are related to solar panels, savings, and how solar works.

  • Why decide to install solar?

    We don't need to convince you to spend money on electricity . Either way you will be spending money on electricity. However, you become your own power company, and this time you have control over the rates.

    Going solar is the difference between owning your electricity, or renting your electricity. Everyone knows it’s better to own a home than to rent a home, because of the equity you build. Solar is the same concept, plus, you save money on your monthly bill!

  • What is a fixed solar bill?

    If you qualify, there is no out of pocket cost or down payments. With current legislation, there is solar Incentive Tax Credit for residential system owners at up to 30% of the total system cost.

  • Is solar expensive?

    If you qualify, there is no out of pocket cost or down payments. With current legislation, there is a solar Incentive Tax Credit for residential system owners at up to 30% of the total system cost.

  • What happens when I sell my home?

    When you sell your home, the panels stay with the home, and there is no hassle to transfer ownership. When you sell your home you sell the system and cash out on the equity you built, but now the new homeowner has the payment, and you can go solar on your next home just as hassle free as we did it for you the first time

  • How does a home solar system work?

    A home solar system is made up of components that all work together to convert the power of sunlight into usable energy for your lights, appliances and devices.

  • Will I ever have to pay a power bill?

    No because your solar system will produce all the electricity for your home and you won't have to buy from the utility company. All you’ll pay is a small connection fee of $3-13.

  • What is installed as a part of a solar system?

    Each home is different, and the equipment we install can vary from house to house. Systems range from panels, to batteries, to generators, to inverters, etc.
